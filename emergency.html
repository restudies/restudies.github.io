You’re not okay. You know that. But no one seems to take it seriously — because your body still moves, your mouth still works, and your eyes still fake presence.

Inside, though? Something broke. Not just depression. Not just anxiety. Something deeper.

The feeling that you don’t know who you are anymore. Or worse — that you never did.

That’s what this book is about.

It’s not a memoir. It’s not self-help. It’s a black box recording of a psychic crash. The wreckage of identity.

You won’t find steps. You won’t find platitudes. But you will find something human, raw, and true. Something that might echo where you're stuck. Something that might keep you breathing.

Because this isn’t about finding out who you are. It’s about finally feeling what’s left when the lies fall off.

You won’t read this like a story. You’ll read it like you’re combing through the aftermath, trying to understand what happened to you through someone else’s wreckage.

And somewhere in it, you might find the moment when the collapse turned quiet, and the quiet turned real.

Reading This Book

This isn’t a traditional narrative. It’s not structured around events or lessons. It’s shaped by turning points — those moments when something cracked, broke, shifted, or revealed itself.

Some chapters span a lifetime. Some focus on a single second. What matters isn’t how long they are, but how true they feel.

If you’re lost, hollow, or numb, don’t expect to be rescued here. Expect to be met.

Not with instructions. But with language that might name what you haven’t been able to say. And maybe that’s what survival really needs.

This isn’t about answers. This is about what stays when the asking burns away.

What remains when you stop trying to fix yourself, and just sit with what’s still alive inside.

That is the real beginning.
